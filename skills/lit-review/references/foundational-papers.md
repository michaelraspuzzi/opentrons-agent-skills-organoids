# Key Papers in Cardiac Organoid Research

## Must-Know Papers (Cite These)

### Foundational Protocols (2017-2021)

**Mills et al., 2017** — PNAS
"Functional screening in human cardiac organoids reveals a metabolic mechanism for cardiomyocyte cell cycle arrest"
- DOI: 10.1073/pnas.1707316114
- **Key contribution**: Established metabolic maturation via fatty acid switch
- **Why it matters**: Foundation for all maturation approaches
- Lab: Mills/Hudson (QIMR)

**Hofbauer et al., 2021** — Cell
"Cardioids reveal self-organizing principles of human cardiogenesis"
- DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2021.04.034
- **Key contribution**: First self-organizing chamber-forming cardioids
- **Why it matters**: Proved organoids can form cavities without scaffolds
- Lab: Mendjan (IMBA)

**Lewis-Israeli et al., 2021** — Nature Communications
"Self-assembling human heart organoids for the modeling of cardiac development and congenital heart disease"
- DOI: 10.1038/s41467-021-25329-5
- **Key contribution**: Reproducible 96-well protocol matching fetal transcriptome
- **Why it matters**: Standard protocol for developmental studies
- Lab: Aguirre (MSU)

### Recent Breakthroughs (2023-2025)

**Schmidt et al., 2023** — Cell
"Multi-chamber cardioids unravel human heart development and cardiac defects"
- DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2023.10.030
- **Key contribution**: First multi-chamber model (LV, RV, atria, OFT, AVC)
- **Why it matters**: Can now study chamber-specific defects and coordination
- Lab: Mendjan (IMBA)

**Volmert et al., 2023** — Nature Communications
"A patterned human primitive heart organoid model generated by pluripotent stem cell self-organization"
- DOI: 10.1038/s41467-023-43999-1
- **Key contribution**: Anterior-posterior patterning, proepicardial formation
- **Why it matters**: More physiological developmental recapitulation
- Lab: Aguirre (MSU)

**Pocock et al., 2025** — Nature Cardiovascular Research
"Maturation of human cardiac organoids enables complex disease modeling and drug discovery"
- DOI: 10.1038/s44161-025-00669-3
- **Key contribution**: AMPK/ERR-driven maturation; DSP cardiomyopathy model
- **Why it matters**: Most mature organoids for drug screening; found therapeutic
- Lab: Mills/Hudson (QIMR)

**Voges et al., 2023** — Cell Reports
"Vascular cells improve functionality of human cardiac organoids"
- DOI: 10.1016/j.celrep.2023.112322
- **Key contribution**: Vascularization improves maturation via LAMA5
- **Why it matters**: Mechanistic understanding of vascular-cardiac crosstalk
- Lab: Mills/Hudson (QIMR)

**Kostina et al., 2024** — Stem Cell Reports
"ER stress and lipid imbalance drive diabetic embryonic cardiomyopathy in an organoid model"
- DOI: 10.1016/j.stemcr.2024.01.003
- **Key contribution**: Diabetes-CHD mechanism; omega-3 therapeutic
- **Why it matters**: First organoid-based therapeutic discovery for CHD
- Lab: Aguirre (MSU)
